Session Tips

1. High value food is essential! Bring as many treats as you possibly can. Especially those {{dog.first_name}} go nuts for. This will help us get the very best expressions and make them happy to work for us. If toys or balls are popular with your pooch, pack the favourites.

2. Wrap up well if its forecast to be chilly or wet and pack some water for {{dog.first_name}} if its likely to be warm. We all know the weather can be unpredictable and change at the last minute, but don't worry - we are well equipped to deal with all sorts of weather conditions, with the exception of torrential rain - in which case we would contact you to re-arrange the appointment.

3. The session is all about your fur-family, but if you'd like to be in a couple of portraits with them we'd be more than happy to oblige. If you are going to join in on the photos its best to avoid clothing with large text or branding and to make sure that the colours or patterns you wear compliment your dog. Drop us a line if you'd like help with this.

4. Bring a lead and collar - especially if your furry companion usually wears a harness. While harness' worn during the session will be visible in the final images, we can remove leads. *Spoiler alert* ..... most of the images that you see on our website were dogs on a lead! We totally get that your doggo might be a wiggly bum or a little timid. Rest assured, most of the dogs we work with stay on a lead for the majority of the session. We simply capture a split second moment and remove the lead in post-processing.

5. Keep your fur-family clean and dry prior to the session. If you want to take them for a walk beforehand, make sure there's a good few hours until the session starts so they have time to clean up and get their energy levels back. Even if your pooch is the energetic type, the session is a lot of mental simulation and fun for your pooch and we want them to be firing on all cylinders!

6. Last but not least - stay calm and have fun! Dogs are incredibly intuitive, reactive beings and pick up on their owners emotions. We promise that whatever happens we will successfully photograph your dog and and have great fun with it.
